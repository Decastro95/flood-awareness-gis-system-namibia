# 2011 Floods Impact on Human Security: Oshoopala Case Study

Author: Sarafina Eine Xungileni Tshilunga (2014)

## Abstract
Study on 2011 floods' effects on Oshoopala... no deaths, but disruptions to education, health, food, livelihoods.

## Background
Oshoopala: 3,000 inhabitants, low-lying area. Recurrent floods since 2008.

## Findings
- Education: 6 schools closed, 2,204 learners affected.
- Health: Poor drainage led to illnesses.
- Livelihoods: Self-employed lost businesses.
- Vulnerability: Situated in 'oshana' pan.

| Effects | Hazard Factors | Effects |
|---------|----------------|---------|
| Poor drainage | Stagnant water | Diseases, mosquito breeding |

## Conclusions
Floods persist; need relocation, awareness.

## Recommendations
Assess habitability, community involvement, proactive strategies.

Appendices: Questionnaires for residents.
